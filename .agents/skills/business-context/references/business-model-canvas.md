# Business Model Canvas Reference

## Purpose

Framework for analyzing and designing business models. Use during Opportunity Discovery to understand business context and identify Viability risks.

## The 9 Components

### 1. Customer Segments
- Who are we creating value for?
- Segment types: Mass market, Niche market, Segmented, Diversified, Multi-sided

### 2. Value Propositions
- What value do we deliver?
- Value drivers: Newness, Performance, Customization, Design, Brand/Status, Price, Cost reduction, Risk reduction, Accessibility, Convenience/Usability

### 3. Channels
- How do customers discover, evaluate, purchase, receive, and get support?
- Channel phases: Awareness → Evaluation → Purchase → Delivery → After-sales

### 4. Customer Relationships
- What relationship does each segment expect?
- Types: Personal assistance, Dedicated support, Self-service, Automated, Community, Co-creation

### 5. Revenue Streams
- What value are customers willing to pay for?
- Types: Asset sale, Usage fee, Subscription, Lending/Leasing, Licensing, Brokerage, Advertising

### 6. Key Resources
- What do our value propositions require?
- Types: Physical, Intellectual, Human, Financial

### 7. Key Activities
- What must we do to deliver value?
- Types: Production, Problem solving, Platform/network

### 8. Key Partnerships
- Who are key partners/suppliers?
- Types: Strategic alliances, Coopetition, Joint ventures, Buyer-supplier

### 9. Cost Structure
- What are the most important costs?
- Types: Cost-driven, Value-driven
- Characteristics: Fixed, Variable, Economies of scale/scope

## Application in nautilus

### Discovery Phase
- Use BMC to understand the **business context** of an Opportunity
- **Viability risk** assessment: Does the Solution fit within the business model?
- Identify revenue model implications of proposed Solutions

### Common Patterns

The following patterns are illustrative examples, not an exhaustive list of valid business models.

**SaaS**: Subscription revenue, platform as key resource, specific industry segments
**Marketplace**: Multi-sided segments, transaction fees, platform management as key activity
**Freemium**: Free tier + paid premium, self-service + upselling, conversion optimization

### Revenue Model Considerations
- Pricing strategy: Value-based, Cost-plus, Market-based
- Revenue timing: One-time, Recurring, Usage-based
- Payment model: Freemium, Subscription, Tiered, Pay-per-use

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] All 9 components addressed
- [ ] Customer segments clearly defined
- [ ] Value proposition specific and compelling
- [ ] Revenue streams realistic
- [ ] Cost structure viable
- [ ] Model internally consistent
- [ ] MVP-compatible (scalable from minimal to full)
